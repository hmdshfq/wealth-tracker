## Common Questions

### Why do the bands get wider over time?

This is normal! Over longer time horizons, small differences in returns compound significantly, creating greater uncertainty. This doesn't mean the projection is wrong - it reflects the reality of long-term investing.

### Should I be worried if my projection drops below the median?

Not necessarily. Short-term fluctuations are normal. Focus on the long-term trend and whether your goal remains within the confidence range.

### How often should I check my confidence bands?

We recommend:
- **Monthly:** Quick review with current settings
- **Quarterly:** Adjust volatility based on market conditions
- **Annually:** Comprehensive review with potential goal adjustments

### Can I trust the upper confidence band?

The 90th percentile shows what's possible under favorable conditions, but it's not a guarantee. Think of it as an "optimistic but realistic" scenario rather than a promise.

## Glossary

**Monte Carlo Simulation:** A mathematical technique that runs thousands of random scenarios to predict possible outcomes

**Percentile:** A value below which a certain percentage of observations fall (e.g., 90th percentile = 90% of simulations are below this value)

**Volatility:** A measure of how much investment prices fluctuate over time

**Confidence Band:** The range between two percentiles showing likely outcomes

**Median (50th Percentile):** The middle value - half of simulations are above, half below
